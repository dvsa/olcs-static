Convictions and penalties guidelines

You must tell the Traffic Commissioner about offences that involve:

  • anybody you’ve named in your application
  • companies directed by anybody you’ve named
  • parent companies, if you’re a limited company
Which offences do they need to know about?

The Traffic Commissioner needs to know if anybody you’ve named has been convicted of an offence related to:

  • the Goods Vehicles (Licensing of Operators) Act 1995
  • the Transport Act 1968 or theRoad Traffic Act 1960
  • section 13 of the Hydrocarbon Oil Duties Act 1979
  • sections 53, 74, 173 and 174 of the Road Traffic Act 1988
  • section 2 of the International Road Haulage Permits Act 1975
  • section 3 of the Control of Pollution Act 1974
  • section 2 of the Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978
  • section 1 of the Control of Pollution (Amendment) Act 1989
  • section 33 of the Environmental Protection Act 1990

Spent convictions

Under the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974 you don’t have to declare spent convictions.

Standard National and Standard International licences

If you’re applying for a Standard National or Standard International Licence you must tell the Traffic Commissioner about offences outside the UK.

You should also tell them about any convictions where the punishment was either:

  • imprisonment for longer than three months
  • a fine above level four on the standard scale
  • a community service order (or the equivalent) involving unpaid work for more than 60 hours
